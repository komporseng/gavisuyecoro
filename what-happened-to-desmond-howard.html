What happened to Desmond Howard?

June 18, 2024
blog

Desmond Kevin Howard, a former football wide receiver and return specialist, has had a remarkable career in football, from his college days to his professional career and later transition into broadcasting.

College Career

Desmond Howard's football journey began during his college years at the University of Michigan from 1988 to 1991. He made history at Michigan by becoming the first receiver in Big Ten history to lead the conference in scoring.

He set or tied five NCAA records and 12 single-season Michigan records. In 1991, Howard won the prestigious Heisman Trophy with an outstanding performance, finishing with 950 receiving yards and 19 touchdown passes.

 

Professional Career

The Washington Redskins selected Desmond Howard as the fourth overall pick in the 1992 NFL Draft. He spent 11 seasons in the National Football League, playing for several teams, including the Washington Redskins, Jacksonville Jaguars, Green Bay Packers, Oakland Raiders, and Detroit Lions.

Notably, with the Green Bay Packers, Howard made history by being named the Most Valuable Player of Super Bowl XXXI.

His standout performance included setting an NFL record for punt return yards and returning a kickoff for a 99-yard touchdown, which was the longest return in Super Bowl history at that time.

Howard's contributions to the sport earned him recognition as the only special teams player to receive the Super Bowl MVP award.

His impact on the field was further demonstrated by holding the NFL single-season record for punt return yardage and an impressive total of 8 return touchdowns.

Broadcasting Career

After his remarkable 11 seasons in the NFL, Desmond Howard transitioned into a successful broadcasting career.

He joined ESPN in 2005 as a college football analyst and has been an integral part of the network's Emmy-Award winning show, College GameDay Built by The Home Depot, since then.

Howard's expertise and insights as an analyst have made him a familiar face on various ESPN shows and platforms, including Get Up, SportsCenter, and College Football Live.

Recognition and Inductions

Desmond Howard's impact on the football world extends beyond his playing and broadcasting career. In 2010, he was inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame, recognizing his exceptional contributions during his college years.

Furthermore, Howard's outstanding achievements have led to his induction into various prestigious halls of fame, including the Gator Bowl Hall of Fame, the University of Michigan Hall of Honor, the State of Michigan Sports Hall of Fame, and the Cleveland Sports Hall of Fame. 

Personal Life

Desmond Howard, born on May 15, 1970, in Cleveland, Ohio, holds a bachelor's degree in mass communications from the University of Michigan. He currently resides in Miami, where he continues to be involved in various football-related activities and his broadcasting career.

 

Lastly, Desmond Howard's journey from his college football days to his successful professional career and later transition into broadcasting has left an indelible mark on the world of football.

His exceptional talent, accolades, and contributions to the sport have solidified his legacy as a celebrated figure in football history.
