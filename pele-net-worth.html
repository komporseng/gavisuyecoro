Who is Pele?

Pelé, the legendary Brazilian footballer, left an indelible mark on the sports world through his incredible achievements. He’s hailed for winning three FIFA World Cups in 1958, 1962, and 1970, a feat unmatched by any other player. His talent was apparent from the start, joining Santos FC at 15 and making his mark on the Brazil national team at just 16. His playing style mesmerized fans and brought him and his team immense fame.

At Santos, playing with legendary players like Zito, Pepe, and Coutinho, Pelé displayed his extraordinary abilities and broke records that are still in place. His ability to score goals was exceptional; in 694 league games and 1363 games overall, he scored an incredible 650 goals. He even had the distinction of the highest-paid athlete in the world during the height of his career.

Pelé continued to be a representation of Brazilian football excellence even after he retired in 1977. He was still held in high regard, winning several individual and team honors for his outstanding accomplishments and influence on the game. His status as one of the all-time great football players solidifies his position in Brazilian sports history.

Pele biography

Pelé, born on October 23, 1940, turned 82 in 2022. Contrary to the belief, he wasn’t raised in a wealthy family in Tres Coracoes, Brazil; instead, he grew up in more humble circumstances. He’s Brazilian by birth and holds a Christian faith. His schooling ended early, but he later pursued higher education at Brazil’s Metropolitan University of Santos, although his passion for sports always outweighed his interest in academics right from childhood.

In the realm of relationships, Pele’s life has seen various chapters. He was married to Assiria Lemos Seixas before marrying Marcia in 1994, though their marriage ended in divorce in 2008. Prior to Assiria, he was married to Rosemeri dos Reis Cholbi in 1966, but they divorced in 1982. Additionally, he had a relationship with Xuxa Meneghel from 1981 to 1986.

Pelé is a father to six children: a son named Joshua Nascimento and five daughters—Sandra Regina Arantes do Nascimento, Celeste Nascimento, Flavia Christina Kurtz Nascimento, Kelly Critina Nascimento, and Jennifer Nascimento.

Family Background

This legendary football player was born on October 23, 1940, in Tres Coracoes, Minas Gerais, Brazil. Joao Ramos do Nascimento, his father, was formerly a player for Fluminense. Pele had to work as a tea stand attendant to make ends meet while growing up in poverty. He is the eldest child of Joao Ramos and Celeste Arantes. Zeca and Maria Lucia Nascimento are his brothers and sisters, respectively. Although his family named him Deco, he was given the moniker Pele at school.

Career

Pele, the renowned former Brazilian football maestro, hails from Sao Paulo, Brazil. Widely celebrated as one of the greatest players ever, FIFA itself bestowed upon him the title of “the greatest.” Pele’s remarkable career saw him achieve a world record by scoring an astounding 1279 goals in 1363 matches, a feat that solidified his status as a football legend.

As a key player for Brazil, he also shares the distinction of being the joint-highest goalscorer, netting 77 goals in 92 matches. His unique achievement of winning the FIFA World Cup three times—in 1958, 1962, and 1970—remains unparalleled, marking him as the only player to accomplish such a remarkable feat.

Beyond the football field, Pele took on roles as a UNESCO Goodwill Ambassador in 1994 and an Extraordinary Minister for Sport in 1995. In his personal life, Pele experienced three marriages. His first, in 1966, was with Rosemeri dos Reis Cholbi, resulting in two daughters and one son, but the marriage ended in divorce in 1982. His second marriage, in 1994 to Brazilian singer Assíria Nascimento, also ended in divorce in 2008. Finally, in 2016, he tied the knot with Brazilian entrepreneur Marcia Aoki, marking a new chapter in his personal life.
