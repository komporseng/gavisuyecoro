Jujimufu Wiki Bio

Jon Call was born in the USA on 9 December 1985 so under the zodiac sign of Sagittarius and holding American nationality – better known online as Jujimufu, he is popular for his career of a weight lifter.

Childhood and education

Jujimufu hasn’t talked much about his parents as he respects their privacy as much as his own, but he has shared that they have been very supportive of his career of a weight lifter – he hasn’t mentioned having any siblings, which is why he is considered to be an only child.

Jujimufu’s journey began at the age of 13 when his parents enrolled him in Taekwondo. Learning that he wasn’t in shape to be good enough at it, his parents paid a gym membership for him – the gym was right across the street from his school and he would go there every day after school to work out.

When he was 15, he learned about doing tricks, and found tutorials on the internet – he used his backyard as the place for his exercises, but soon learned that he wasn’t flexible enough for them. He began doing flexibility exercises every night for two hours, and it took him two months to obtain the flexibility he wanted.

After he matriculated from high school, Jujimufu enrolled at college and graduated four years later with a bachelor’s degree.

Career of a bodybuilder and other jobs

Jujimufu’s career was officially launched in 2002, when he launched his tricktutorials.com website, using it to show people how to do tricks, and combine them with bodybuilding and other things.

He rose to prominence after he appeared in the 11th season of the “America’s Got Talent” show, but only passed to the second round. His well-built figure helped Jujimufu become a sought-after mode,l as he can often be seen in underwear commercials and advertisements, while he has also posed for numerous fitness magazines, and has worked with some of the most popular clothing and watch brands.

Jujimufu is a writer as well, and has so far released two books, entitled “Legendary Flexibility” and “Train 365”. He worked as a technical manager for a biotechnology group for more than seven years before deciding to leave the job in December 2016, and focus on his fitness career.

He and his friend Tom Boyden have since founded several businesses and social platforms, which are working better than expected. Jujimufu is a founder of acrobolix, which is a mixed technique combining acrobatic moves with bodybuilding.

Jujimufu launched his YouTube channel on 13 July 2008, in a collaboration with his friend Tom Boyden – there are currently more than 950,000 people subscribed to it, which also counts more than 150 million views of all their videos combined. They mostly upload funny videos related to exercising and weight lifting.

Jujimufu’s training and dieting

Jujimufu has five training sessions every week which usually last between one and two hours – he used 2018 for an experiment as he worked out every day without any days off (straight 365 days).

When it comes to his exercises, he does all the moves he loves during his skill-work, however, when it is about bodybuilding exercises, he is mostly focused on those he isn’t good at, pectoral and biceps development due to genetics, so he works more on them than on other parts of his body.

The diet he follows is called a generic body builder diet – it includes six meals per day which are all high in protein. He eats a lot of carbohydrates, and is trying to reduce his intake of fat as much as he can – his daily protein-carbohydrate-fat split is 40:40:20. While other bodybuilders and fitness icons might recommend these foods, Jujimufu avoids oats, wheat, peanut butter and milk while he eats plenty of garlic, ginger root, lemons, rosemary, cinnamon, vegetables and fresh fruits.
